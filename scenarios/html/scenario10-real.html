ðŸ§  Key Lesson: Signs of Good Data Visualization

When evaluating any chart or graph, look for these signs of honest presentation:

  • Y-axis starts at zero (for bar charts and most line charts)
  • Clear source citation - who collected this data?
  • Reputable organization - is the source trustworthy?
  • Baseline comparison - context like "average for all occupations"
  • Appropriate chart type - bar chart for comparing categories
  • No cherry-picked timeframes - is the full picture shown?
  • Percentages add up - pie charts must equal 100%
  • Proper axis orientation - for rankings, lower should be "up"
