ðŸ§  Key Lesson: Rankings Need Inverted Axes

When charting rankings, lower numbers are BETTER (1st beats 2nd). A properly designed chart would either:

  • Invert the Y-axis so 1st place is at the top
  • Or clearly label that "down" means "improvement"

This chart exploits our instinct that "up = good" and "down = bad" to make Ireland's best Olympic performance ever look like a disaster!
